BEIJING — Did China’s maternal mortality rate surge by nearly one-third in the first half of 2016, as a top health official reportedly said this week?
北京——据报道，一位高级卫生官员本周表示，2016年上半年中国孕产妇死亡率升高近三分之一。事实真的是这样吗？
An anxious, skeptical debate has broken out over the drastically higher figure, announced by Ma Xiaowei, deputy director of the National Health and Family Planning Commission, at a meeting on Tuesday on improving maternal health, in reports carried widely in the Chinese news media.
中文新闻媒体广泛报道，周二当天，中国国家卫生和计划生育委员会副主任马晓伟在一次以加强孕产妇健康为主题的会议上公布了一个大幅升高的数字。围绕这个令人担忧并受到质疑的数字爆发了一场讨论。
Online, people called for better health care for pregnant women or warned of the health effects of pregnancy on “old mothers” who may have rushed to have a second child after the end of the one-child policy on Jan. 1.
在网上，人们呼吁为孕妇提供更好的保健服务，或是告诫在今年1月1日一孩政策结束后赶着生二胎的“高龄妈妈”，怀孕会对健康有哪些影响。
Older mothers may face greater health risks, but not necessarily. Chinese women generally marry and have children in their late 20s, contributing to suspicion among some of the appropriateness of older women having children.
高龄妈妈可能会面临更大的健康风险，但这并非必然。中国女性普遍会在快30岁时结婚生子，这导致一些人怀疑，超过这个年龄的女性不适合生孩子。
Here are the highlights of what Mr. Ma said at the meeting, as reported by Beijing News and Yicai, a Shanghai-based business media group also known as China Business Network:
以下是《新京报》和总部设在上海、英文名为China Business Network的商业媒体集团第一财经报道的马晓伟讲话的主要内容：
■ Maternal mortality in 2015 was 20.1 per 100,000 women.
■ 2015年孕产妇死亡率为20.1/10万。
■ In the first six months of 2016, it was 18.3.
■ 2016年上半年，孕产妇死亡率为18.3/10万。
■ There was an increase of 30.6 percent in the first half of 2016 over the same period in 2015.
■ 2016年上半年，全国孕产妇死亡率比去年同期增长30.6%。
In an article in its health section, Sohu.com quoted Mr. Ma as saying that the increase was “an urgent problem” and calling for greater investment in hospitals, maternal health care and in training obstetricians in an era of two children.
搜狐健康频道的一篇文章援引马晓伟的话表示，这样的增长是个“急迫的问题”，他呼吁在二胎时代对医院、孕产期保健及产科医生培训方面进行更多的投资。
“Fishy,” said Wang Ling, the author of “A Beijing Pregnancy” (“北京孕事”), a novel about pregnancy, abortion and birth, on Population Internal Reference, her private WeChat site. Ms. Wang, who uses the pen name Lingzi, did not immediately respond to a request for an interview.
王羚是一本有关怀孕、流产和生育的小说《北京孕事》的作者，使用笔名羚子。她在自己的微信公号“人口内参”上表示，这个数据“不对劲”，但她没有立即回应时报的采访请求。
“Under what circumstances could the rate be 30 percent higher in the first half of this year than in the same period last year, if last year’s overall maternal mortality rate was 20 and this year’s so far is 18?” Ms. Wang asked.
“今年上半年十万分之18，去年是十万分之20，怎样的情况下才能比去年同期增长30%呢？”王羚问。
Such an increase in the first half of 2016 could be achieved only if the rate had been a very low 14 per 100,000 in the first half of last year and a very high 26 per 100,000 women in the second half, according to figures on Ms. Wang’s WeChat account.
王羚微信公号上的数据显示，只有去年上半年的孕产妇死亡率只有非常低的14/10万，而下半年达到非常高的26/10万，2016年上半年才会出现这样幅度的增长。
The commission did not reply to a request for clarification.
中国国家卫生和计划生育委员会没有回应澄清这个问题的请求。
China has experienced drastic improvements in maternal mortality in recent decades.
在最近几十年，中国的孕产妇死亡率有了极大的改善。
Health commission figures show a fall in maternal mortality to 20.1 per 100,000 women in 2015 from 88.8 in 1990.
国家卫生和计划生育委员会的数据显示，中国孕产妇死亡率已经从1990年的88.8/10万，降至2015年的20.1/10万。
The World Health Organization says maternal mortality in the country in 2015 was 27 per 100,000 live births. But it was not clear if the Chinese figure had been calculated using the same standard of live births. Like the Chinese figures, the W.H.O.’s numbers show a marked decline over all, from 97 per 100,000 in 1990.
世界卫生组织（World Health Organization，简称WHO）表示，中国的孕产妇死亡率为每10万个活产婴儿有27名孕产妇死亡。但并不清楚中国的数据是否是使用同样的活产婴儿标准来统计。和中国的数据一样，WHO的数据也显示出，中国的孕产妇死亡率整体上出现明显的下降，从1990年的97/10万降至27/10万。
The W.H.O. gives the maternal mortality rate in the United States as 14 per 100,000 live births for 2015, up from 12 in 1990.
WHO还提供了美国的孕产妇死亡率变化情况，即从1990年的12/10万增加到2015年的14/10万。
Rates in sub-Saharan Africa are the highest in the world, with 882 women dying per 100,000 live births in the Central African Republic, according to the W.H.O.
据WHO统计，非洲撒哈拉沙漠以南地区的孕产妇死亡率是世界上最高的，中非共和国每诞生10万个活产婴儿，就会有882名孕产妇死亡。
Some Chinese news reports tied the apparent surge in maternal deaths to the two-child policy, after 36 years when most urban families were restricted to one child.
中国的一些新闻报道将孕产妇死亡率的显著增高与二胎政策联系了起来。过去30年，中国大多数城市都是实行严格的一胎政策。
“After the ‘two-child policy,’ maternal mortality rises by one-third,” read a headline on Ifeng.com, the website of Phoenix Television.
“全面两孩后，孕产妇死亡率同比上涨3成，”凤凰卫视的网站上转载的一篇文章的标题写道。
That view apparently struck a chord among many online readers.
这种观点显然引发了许多网友的共鸣。
“This certainly has to do with lots of old women trying desperately to have a child,” wrote gernsback-ex, the most popular comment on Ifeng.com.
“这个和很多高龄妇女拼命生孩子肯定有关，”名为gernsback-ex的用户在凤凰网上发布的最热评论如此写道。
But Yicai disagreed, with a headline that ran in part: “Scholars say it has nothing to do with the two-child policy.”
但第一财经不同意这种观点，其报道在标题中写道，“学者称与全面二孩无关”。
If the one-child policy ended on Jan. 1, it could not have caused the spike, because it is not possible to have a child within six months, the article said.
这篇报道还提到，既然一胎政策是于今年1月1日终止，应该不至于引起这么大幅度的上涨，因为六个月时间里孩子还不会出生。
On her WeChat account, Ms. Wang wrote: “If it really has gone up by 30 percent (it’s a frightening figure), then the health commission must offer a clear answer why. And how to avoid it going forward.”
王羚在她的微信公号上写道：“如果真的上升30%（这是一个可怕的数字），卫计委就必须给出一个清晰负责的解释，到底什么原因导致？如何在下一步避免这种状况？”
