Before saying goodbye to the global blitz of “super blood moon” eclipse photography and chatter (already supplanted by the momentous NASA confirmation of signs that water flows on present-day Mars), I wanted to post a short note I received Monday morning from Deborah Hamon, an environment-focused artist from Marin County, Calif.
在和全球铺天盖地的有关“超级红月亮”月食的照片和讨论（已经被NASA证实有迹象表明目前的火星表面有水流动这一重大事件所取代）说再见之前，我想把周一上午收到的一条短信公布出来。这条短信来自加利福尼亚州马林县的德博拉·哈蒙(Deborah Hamon)。她是一名关注环境的艺术家。
Hamon compares the powerful lunar pull that prompted so many millions to look skyward Sunday night to a sustainability researcher’s comment about the lack of a human “cultural narrative” for planetary care in my coverage of Pope Francis. Here’s how she put it:
周日晚上，月亮的强大吸引力促使数以百万计的民众仰望天空。哈蒙将这种吸引力和一位可持续发展研究人员的评论做了比较。这名研究人员称，在我对教皇方济各(Pope Francis)的报道中，缺少人类爱护地球的“文化叙事”。以下是哈蒙的原话：
I just came in from viewing the super moon eclipse, which ended up being quite a gathering in my neighborhood, including a seniors’ ukelele band called “The Fountain of Ukes”singing songs about the moon and more….
我刚看完超级月食回到屋子里。这次月食成就了我所在社区的一场盛大聚会，其间有一个叫“尤克里里喷泉”的老年尤克里里乐队唱着和月亮有关的歌曲……
But as I was watching this amazing show put on by the earth, moon and sun, I thought about all the people in my neighborhood, all the people across the country, and all the people in the part of the world where it was visible, who were coming together at the same time to watch this event and marvel and talk about it. Then I read your piece, and I just thought why is it so difficult to manage our planet, when all these people can come together simultaneously to celebrate the moon. Cultural narrative indeed…
但在观看这场由地球、月球和太阳合作的精彩演出时，我想到的是我所在这个社区的人，全国各地的人，以及世界上能看到这次月食的所有人。他们在同一时刻，一起观看和讨论这次月食，并发出惊叹声。接着我看了你的文章。我就在想，为什么大家能同时聚在一起赏月，但管理我们的地球却这么难。的确是文化叙事……
That is indeed a question worth pondering.
这的确是个值得深思的问题。
