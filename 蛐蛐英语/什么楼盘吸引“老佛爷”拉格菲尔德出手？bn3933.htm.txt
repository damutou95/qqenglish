It is hard to imagine what Germany’s wartime armed forces and Karl Lagerfeld could have in common. But the answer can be found in an austere neo-Classical edifice that stands in the most affluent district of this northern German city.
很难想象德国的战时军队与卡尔·拉格菲尔德(Karl Lagerfeld)有什么共同之处。不过，答案可以在位于文中这座德国北方城市最富城区内的一栋朴实无华的新古典主义大宅中找到。
The building, the Sophienpalais, was constructed in 1937 as a headquarters for Hitler’s armed forces. It continued to be used as a military complex until 2006 and today is being turned into 105 apartments, part of the Sophienterrassen project.
该建筑名为Sophienpalais，始建于1937年，最初是希特勒的军队总部。直到2006年以前，它一直被当做军用设施。如今，它正被改建成包含105套公寓的住宅楼。改建工程是Sophienterrassen项目的一部分。
Enter Mr. Lagerfeld, who was born in Hamburg in 1933. The creative force behind Chanel and Fendi, he is designing a very unmilitary lobby and spa for the project, and can even be commissioned to bring his stylish touch to individual apartments. (The developer, Frankonia, will not disclose Mr. Lagerfeld’s fee.)
接下来讲讲拉格菲尔德。他1933年出生于汉堡。这位香奈儿(Chanel)和芬迪(Fendi)背后的创作生力军，正在为Sophienterrassen项目设计极力淡化军事色彩的大厅和温泉浴场。他甚至有可能受托，以其独特的时尚风格装点大宅内的单套公寓（开发商Frankonia拒绝透露拉格菲尔德的佣金）。
In addition to the palace apartments, work is almost complete on five villas and 26 townhouses built on 11 acres, or 4.45 hectares, of adjacent land, bringing the project’s total to 191 residential units.
除大宅内部的公寓之外，Sophienterrassen项目还包括相邻11英亩（或4.45公顷）地皮上的建筑，包括目前已近完工的五栋独栋别墅(villa)和26座联排别墅(townhouse)，使Sophienterrassen项目的别墅总量达到191套。
The development is about three kilometers from Hamburg’s city center, in Harvestehude, a district of about two square kilometers and 18,000 people that stands along Alster Lake and whose palatial mansions have been reported to house more millionaires than anywhere else in Germany.
此处开发项目距汉堡市中心大约3公里，地处Harvestehude区，该区位于阿尔斯特湖(Alster Lake)湖滨，面积2平方公里，人口18000人。区内富丽堂皇的豪宅鳞次栉比。据报道，居住该区的百万富豪数位居德国之首。
The company’s chief executive, Uwe Schmitz, thought that Sophienterrassen was a “tremendous place to build” and was so enthused that in 2006 he bought the land from the government without first obtaining planning permission. The company’s proposal was later approved.
Frankonia公司的首席执行官乌韦·施密茨(Uwe Schmitz)认为，Sophienterrassen是一块“绝佳的建设用地”，他对这块地皮极其看好，以至于在2006年，他还没拿到规划许可时，就先从政府手里将它买了下来。Frankonia提交的开发计划稍后获得了批准。
His eagerness may have been understandable, since individual properties rarely come on the market in Harvestehude, let alone a palace and 11 acres.
施密茨对这块地皮的热衷，或许不难理解。因为在Harvestehude区很少会有个人住宅类房产上市，更不用说还是一栋带有11英亩（4.45公顷）土地的豪宅了。
“The only way to be able to live here has been by inheriting a house,” said Hilke Branding-Rettig, managing sales director for Frankonia Eurobau. “The rich have kept the homes to themselves.”
“要想在这里居住，从来都只有一种方法，那就是继承一处房产，”房地产公司Frankonia Eurobau的销售主管希尔克·布兰丁-雷蒂希(Hilke Branding-Rettig)说，“一直以来，富豪们都只把房子留给自己住。”
The entire project, which is forecast to cost about 340 million euros, or about $462 million, is scheduled to open in 2015, although 15 families are already living in their townhouses.
整个项目预计耗资3.4亿欧元，约合4.62亿美元（约人民币28亿元），定于2015年开盘，只不过目前有15户人家已经住进联排别墅中了。
Forty-six of the apartments in the Sophienpalais were sold before construction began, to an international group of buyers from Russia, the United Arab Emirates, Egypt and Asia. These early purchasers have paid from �530,000 for a unit of 60 square meters, or 646 square feet, unit to �4.68 million for one of 300 square meters.
Sophienpalais大宅内有46套别墅在动工前便已售出，买家来自俄罗斯、阿联酋、埃及和亚洲。这批最早的置业者，少则支付53万欧元（约合人民币433万元），购买60平方米（或646平方英尺）的公寓单元，多则豪掷468万欧元（约合人民币3824万元），拿下300平方米的单元。
The marketing of the project has emphasized Frankonia’s attention to detail, with its website including a quotation from the English poet John Ruskin: “Quality is never an accident, it is always the result of intelligent effort.”
Sophienpalais的营销活动着力展现Frankonia对细节的关注，其官网上引用了英国诗人约翰·拉斯金(John Ruskin)的一句话：“卓越的品质从来不是偶然，它一向是灵感与努力的结晶。”
For example, the show apartment in one of the villas is 488 square meters and has all the features of a property intended to attract the international market. But it also has a pair of spectacles left lying on an open book, as if the owner had just left the room for a few minutes. Company executives say it is a small detail designed to emphasize that nothing in the project has been overlooked.
举个例子，Sophienpalais项目中的一栋别墅内，有一套面积488平方米的公寓样板间，所有能吸引国际客户的要素，在这套房产当中都已具备。不仅如此，屋内还特意留了一副眼镜，放在一本摊开的书上，仿佛这家的主人只是暂时离开了几分钟。该公司高管表示，这样的小细节就是为了向观众展示，Sophienpalais项目的每一处细节都为精心安排之作。
“Our chairman is a technical person — he wants to build for the long term, said Ms. Branding-Rettig, the managing sales director. “It’s not enough to look good. There has to be quality, and it has to be functional. The lamps in the doorways of the villas cost �16,000. You wouldn’t do that if you thought about costs. Normally in Germany, you ask an architect to design something. He does a perfect job, only for the builder to say ‘no.’ Our C.E.O. says ‘no’ to that — ‘Let’s do what the architect says.”’
“我们的主席是个重技术的人——他想让公司建造的项目历久弥新，”销售主管布兰丁-雷蒂希说，“建筑光是好看还不够，还要有品质和实用性。安装在独栋别墅门口走道处的灯就花了16000欧元（约合人民币132722元）。如果你只想着省钱，是不会买这些灯具的。一般在德国，你让建筑师去设计个什么东西，建筑师设计出了完美的方案，只等着被开发商否决。我们的首席执行官就不这样——他会说：‘我们就照建筑师的方案做。”’
The architecture of the newly built townhouses owes something to the classic designs of the central London district of Mayfair. And Mr. Schmitz was also influenced by Eduard F. Brinkama, an antiques dealer and real estate agent in Hamburg during the 1900s who had lived in London and introduced its townhouse style to the Harvestehude district.
新建的联排别墅借鉴了伦敦市中心梅菲尔区（Mayfair，伦敦的上流住宅区——译注）的经典建筑设计。施密茨也受到了爱德华·F·布林卡玛(Eduard F. Brinkama)的影响。布林卡玛是汉堡上世纪初的一名古董商及房地产经纪人，曾居住在伦敦。是他将伦敦的联排别墅设计风格带到了Harvestehude区。
In the Sophienterrassen project, the result is a blend of the contemporary and the traditional, with clean lines, tall windows and high ceilings in the townhouses.
由此一来，在Sophienpalais项目中，当代与传统的设计元素交汇碰撞，明朗的线条、挑高的窗户和天花板成为了联排别墅的典型特色。
Most townhouses are divided into four units, but there are also triplexes. Each penthouse has balconies on either side of a dining area, a central living space, one master bedroom and two other bedrooms, all with en suite bathrooms. Two parking spaces are also included, and an elevator travels from the parking area directly to the penthouse entrance.
大多数联排别墅内分为四套公寓单元，也有的一分为三。每套顶层公寓(penthouse)都带两个阳台（分别设在用餐区的两边）、一间中央起居室、一间主卧和两间次卧，卧室全部带有配套浴室，还包括两个停车位。停车场和顶层公寓大门间有直达的电梯。
Townhouse prices range from �1.46 million for three-bedroom units to �4.79 million for six- or seven-bedroom homes of more than 530 square meters.
联排别墅的价格，从三居室的146万欧元（约合人民币1193万元）；到能买下面积超过530平方米、含六到七间卧室住宅的479万欧元（约合人民币3914万元）。
Each villa has seven apartments. The smallest, with three bedrooms in a unit of 160 square meters, are priced at �1.46 million. The largest, with six or seven bedrooms over four floors totaling 530 square meters, is priced at �4.68 million.
每座独栋别墅内有七套公寓。最小的为三居室，面积160平方米，定价146万欧元（约合人民币1211万元）；最大的为总面积530平方米、上下共四层的六或七居室，定价468万欧元（约合人民币3882万元）。
The ground-floor apartments in the villas have a split-level design, with the kitchen and dining area on a level above the living room.
别墅内的底层公寓采用错层设计，厨房和用餐区设在起居室的楼上。
The spacious feeling is enhanced by the high ceilings and large windows with views of the lake, and lavish touches such as silk wallpaper and a leather-clad walk-in closet. These apartments are priced at �7.5 million, and the purchase of the show apartment will include all the furniture.
挑高的天花板、可以眺望湖景的大窗，都强化了房屋的宽敞感受；房间里还增添了一些带有奢华情调的元素，比如丝绸壁纸，和一间带皮革软包的走入式衣橱。这些公寓定价750万欧元（约合人民币6128万元），如果购买样板间，还附送其中的所有家具。
Each of the villas has been given a touch of individuality, although the variations are only in details like the metal work of the balconies and the balustrades. Unusually for Germany, the complex will have a concierge service.
每座独栋别墅都带有其独特的个性，虽然其样式变化仅见于诸如阳台和栏杆上的铁艺这类细节设计。这片住宅群将带有门房服务，这在德国颇为少见。
While Sophienterrassen is one of the most expensive projects in one of the city’s most select districts, there is no shortage of villas or townhouses in Hamburg, many built in the Wilhelminian, or Art Nouveau style, popular from 1880 to 1920.
尽管Sophienterrassen坐落在全市百里挑一的地区、又是汉堡最昂贵的开发项目之一，但汉堡并不缺少独栋或联排的别墅。其中许多建筑为新艺术风格（Wilhelminian，又称Art Nouveau），这一流派在1880至1920年间都非常流行。
On the opposite side of the lake, the Uhlenhorst area has homes, many in red brick and stucco, backing onto the canals that intersect the area’s quiet streets.
湖对岸的Uhlenhorst地区也有住宅，其中许多为红砖灰泥建筑，其背面的条条运河，穿过该区宁静的大街小巷。
“Most of the older properties in Uhlenhorst were spared the destruction of World War II,” said Anita Gärtner of Sotheby’s Realty International. “And today many of the big villas have been split into apartments, which cost between �7,000 and �8,000 per square meter. About 60 percent of the properties are rented for about �20 per square meter a month.”
“Uhlenhorst的多数老房子在二战中免于战火摧毁，”苏富比国际地产(Sotheby’s Realty International)的安妮塔·加特纳(Anita Gärtner)说，“现在，有许多大型独栋别墅已经被分成了一套套小公寓，售价在每平方米7000到8000欧元（约合人民币57194至65365元）。大约有60%的房产是出租房，租金每平方米20欧元（约合人民币163元）。”
Harvestehude and Uhlenhorst represent the long-established wealth of Hamburg, but there are several districts that are considered up and coming (and less expensive to live in), such as St. Georg (where prices average �5,900 per square meter), Hohenfelde (�4,300), Eppendorf (�5.800) and the streets of Mühlenkamp (�5.000).
Harvestehude和Uhlenhorst两区，是汉堡传统富豪区的代表。不过在人们眼里，还有好几个地区正在崛起（而且居住成本更低），比如St. Georg地区（平均房价为每平方米5900欧元，约合人民币48207元），Hohenfelde地区（每平方米4300欧元，约合人民币35134元），Eppendorf地区（每平方米5800欧元，约合人民币47390元），以及Mühlenkamp的街区（每平方米5000欧元，约合人民币40853元）。
In Hamburg, the largest area to be redeveloped since World War II is HafenCity, a series of apartment blocks and commercial spaces that stretch more than half a mile along, and in some areas over, the old harbors.
在汉堡，二战后规模最大的再开发地区是HafenCity，一连串的公寓街区和商业场所，沿着老港口一字排开，有些还位居其上，绵延超过半英里（约合800米）。
Work is continuing in the area and it is somewhat bleak, with few cafes and shops, enlivened mostly by the difference in architectural styles of the various buildings.
该地区的施工仍在继续，某种程度上显得有点荒凉。咖啡馆和商店屈指可数，生机大多来自百花齐放的建筑风格所造成的视觉反差。
In the neighborhood is a red brick and glass building with a 135-square-meter apartment for sale. With three living rooms, two bedrooms and two bathrooms, it is on the market for �1.02 million.
社区里有一座用红砖和玻璃盖成的大楼，其中有一套135平方米的公寓在出售。该公寓包含三间起居室、两间卧室和两间浴室，市场价为102万欧元（约合人民币833万元）。
As for the overall trend in the city’s real estate sales, Ms. Gärtner said: “The demand for city villas and homes is showing an upward trend just as apartments with gardens or roof terraces are wanted more than ever. They are popular with Danish and British investors.”
就汉堡房地产销售的整体趋势而言，加特纳表示：“对于都市独栋别墅和住宅的需求呈现出上升趋势；一如带花园或屋顶平台的公寓，也比以往任何时候都受欢迎。它们很受丹麦和英国投资者的追捧。”
But Ms. Branding-Rettig, the Frankonia managing sales director, noted that “when it comes to property, people in Hamburg go for understatement.”
不过Frankonia的销售主管布兰丁-雷蒂希也指出：“在置业问题上，汉堡人偏向保守。”
“In Munich and Düsseldorf, they are eager to show off their wealth,” she said, “but Hamburgers do not go in for ostentation. They wear their fur inside.”
“在慕尼黑和杜塞尔多夫，人们热衷于炫富，”她说，“不过汉堡人比较实在。他们不喜欢招摇。”
