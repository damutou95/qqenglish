In 1995, Maggie Keswick, an expert on Chinese gardens who was terminally ill with breast cancer, founded an organization with her husband, the architecture critic Charles Jencks, to provide free information and support for people with cancer and their families. The first Maggie’s Centre opened in Edinburgh in November 1996, a year after her death, with services that included guidance in navigating the medical system and advice on where to buy a wig. Now there are 16 centers in Britain and one in Hong Kong. All are on the grounds of cancer hospitals, and share a reliance on design to promote healing and comfort. “Architecture amplifies the message,” Mr. Jencks said. “Maggie’s Centres: A Blueprint for Cancer Care,” on view from Friday through April 25 at the New York School of Interior Design, features centers designed by Frank Gehry (in Dundee), Rem Koolhaas, Richard Rogers, Steven Holl, and Piers Gough with Paul Smith. 1995年，罹患乳腺癌的中式园林专家玛姬·凯西克(Maggie Keswick)与她的丈夫、建筑评论家查尔斯·詹克斯(Charles Jencks)共同成立了一个组织，为癌症患者及其家庭提供免费的信息和支持。第一家铭琪中心(Maggie’s Centre)于1996年11月在爱丁堡开张，时值玛姬去世一年。该中心提供的服务，既包括指导人们如何利用医疗制度，也涵盖了假发购买攻略。如今，铭琪中心已在英国设立了16家，在香港也有一家。它们全部依托癌症医院而设，其共同的设计理念，是以促进病人康复、营造舒适的环境为宗旨。“建筑能放大我们想要传达的信号，”詹克斯表示。一场名为“铭琪中心：癌症照料的蓝图”的主题展，将从周五（3月7日——译注）到4月25日在纽约室内设计学院(New York School of Interior Design)举办。展览的主题是一系列由名家设计的铭琪中心，分别为弗兰克·盖里（Frank Gehry，设计了邓迪[Dundee，苏格兰海港——译注]的铭琪中心）、雷姆·库哈斯(Rem Koolhaas)、理查德·罗杰斯(Richard Rogers)、史蒂芬·霍尔(Steven Holl)，以及皮尔斯·高夫(Piers Gough)和保罗·史密斯(Paul Smith)的搭档组合。详情可洽：212-472-1500，或登录网站nysid.edu。
