Ekaterina Panikanova is a book lover, but not in the traditional sense. A Russian-born artist, she uses the pages of old French and Italian books that she finds at flea markets as her canvases.
叶卡捷琳娜·帕尼卡诺娃(Ekaterina Panikanova)是个书籍爱好者，但不是传统意义上的书虫。这位出生于俄罗斯的艺术家在跳蚤市场上淘到一些法国和意大利的老书，于是把这些书的书页当成了自己的画布。
The wear and tear, and the scribblings of previous owners, inspire the images Ms. Panikanova creates. And if a painting does not work out, she said, she simply rips out the page and starts over.
书页有着饱经沧桑的质感，上面还留着以前的主人随手画下涂鸦，这些都激发着帕尼卡诺娃作画的灵感。帕尼卡诺娃说，如果一幅画的效果不好，她会索性再从书中撕下一页重画。
When Rick Vintage, a founder of the adventurous Dutch wallpaper company NLXL, discovered Ms. Panikanova’s paintings at a gallery in Rome, where she now lives, he knew he wanted to turn her work into wallcoverings: “It gave me goose bumps,” he said.
荷兰有一家富有开拓精神的墙纸公司NLXL。其创始人里克·温塔奇(Rick Vintage)在罗马的一家画廊里发现了帕尼卡诺娃的画作——罗马是帕尼卡诺娃现在的居住地。当时他就知道，自己想把她的作品变成墙纸：“她的作品让我起了鸡皮疙瘩。”他说。
The collection that resulted, called Biblioteca, comes in five designs, for about $300 a role. Information: 212-352-1570 or NLXL.com.
由此诞生的墙纸系列称为“藏书”(Biblioteca)，有五种设计，每卷大约300美元（约合人民币1873元）。详情请咨询：212-352-1570或NLXL.com。
