LONDON – SNS Reaal, a state-owned Dutch financial services company, said on Monday that it had agreed to sell its Vivat Verzekeringen insurance arm to a unit of Anbang Insurance Group of China.
伦敦——荷兰国有金融服务公司SNS Reaal周一表示，已同意将旗下的维瓦保险公司(Vivat Verzekeringen)出售给中国安邦保险集团股份有限公司的下属公司。
The Chinese company will pay 150 million euros, or about $172 million, for all of Vivat’s shares, SNS Reaal said.
SNS Reaal称，这家中国公司将出资1.5亿欧元（约合11亿元人民币），买下维瓦的全部股权。
Anbang would also inject �770 million to �1 billion into Vivat and assume �552 million in debt, according to NL Financial Investments, which manages the Dutch state’s investments in SNS Reaal and other companies nationalized during and after the financial crisis.
据NL金融投资公司(NL Financial Investments)称，安邦也将向维瓦注入7.7亿至10亿欧元资金，并承担5.52亿欧元债务。该公司管理着荷兰政府对SNS Reaal以及其他在金融危机期间和之后被国有化的公司的投资。
“We welcome the investment of Anbang in the Dutch insurance sector,” Gerard van Olphen, the SNS Reaal chairman, said in a news release. “Through the sale, Vivat will fulfill an important role in further expanding the insurance activities of Anbang in the Netherlands.”
“我们欢迎安邦投资荷兰保险业，”SNS Reaal的董事长在新闻稿中说。“在安邦进一步拓展在荷兰的保险业务的过程中，维瓦将通过这笔交易发挥重要作用。”
The deal is subject to regulatory approval and is expected to be completed in the third quarter of this year.
交易还有待监管机构的审批，预计将在今年第三季度完成。
SNS Reaal also said it would reshape its SNS Bank unit into a stand-alone business, most likely by transferring it to the Dutch government at the time of the Vivat transaction.
SNS Reaal也表示将对旗下的SNS银行(SNS Bank)进行重组，使之成为一家独立的企业。该公司极有可能采取的方式，是在进行维瓦这笔交易时，将SNS银行转让给荷兰政府。
In a news release, Anbang said it was confident it could “provide a solid foundation for Vivat, its customers and its staff,” and that the Chinese company could “benefit from the ongoing consolidation in the Dutch insurance sector.”
安邦在新闻稿中表示，相信自己能“为维瓦及其客户和员工提供坚实的基础”，并称安邦将“受益于荷兰保险业正在进行的整合”。
