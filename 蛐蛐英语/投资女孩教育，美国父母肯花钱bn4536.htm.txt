TO THE LIST of expenses that make raising a girl pricey — clothes, makeup, smartphones — add one more item: education.
在养育女孩的花费清单——衣服、化妆品、智能手机——上还得多加一项：教育。
Parents of girls spend, on average, 25 percent more on education per year in a child’s lifetime than parents of boys. That’s a striking reversal from the 1970s, when parents of girls spent about 30 percent less.
平均来说，女孩家长在孩子成长中每年的教育开销，比男孩的家长要高出25%。这种趋势是从20世纪70年代开始发生逆转的，此前，女孩家长的教育开销平均要低30%左右。
“I am still surprised by our findings,” said Sabino Kornrich, a sociologist at Emory and an author of “Investing in Children: Changes in Parental Spending on Children 1972-2007,” a recent study of government data on more than 33,000 families. “We really just thought spending would equalize,” he said. Instead, Dr. Kornrich and his co-author, Frank Furstenberg of the University of Pennsylvania, found that after spending on girls caught up to boys in the 1990s, it surged ahead in the last decade.
“对于我们的发现，我到现在还有点意外，”埃默里大学(Emory)社会学家萨比诺·康里奇(Sabino Kornrich)最近对超过3.3万户家庭的政府数据进行了研究，并发表论文《投资子女：1972-2007年家长开支变迁》，他说，“我们原以为家长的花费会是一样的。”与此相反，康里奇博士和他的合作者、宾州大学(University of Pennsylvania)的弗兰克·弗斯滕伯格(Frank Furstenberg)发现，在20世纪90年代，在女孩身上的教育投资开始赶上男孩，并在过去10年出现峰值。
Parents spend an additional $855 a year on the education of their 18- to 24-year-old daughters. Part of the reason is that young women today are more likely to attend and complete college than boys, meaning more tuition dollars. But expectations of a bigger payoff may also play a part.
对于18-24岁的女儿，家长每年要多花855美元用于教育投资。部分原因在于当代的年轻女性比起男孩，上大学并完成学业的可能性更大，这就意味着更高的学费开支。此外，对于孩子今后收入将更高的预期也起到了一定作用。
“If girls are doing better and getting accepted into better universities on average, or parents feel they have a higher likelihood of graduating,” Dr. Kornrich said, “they just might invest more.”
“如果总体来说，女孩学业表现更优异，能够考进更好的大学，或者家长们觉得她们在顺利毕业方面可能性更大，他们确实有可能投入更多教育资金，”康里奇说。
