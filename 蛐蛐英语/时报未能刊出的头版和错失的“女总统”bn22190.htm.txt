It’s standard practice for journalists everywhere: When facing a major story on deadline, put together material in advance to account for each possible outcome.
这是世界各地记者的标准做法：面临一个重要事件的截稿日期时，提前编排材料，以应对各种可能的结果。
It happens every day at The New York Times — and it happened here Tuesday night.
这种情况每天都在《纽约时报》发生，周二晚上也不外如是。
Various front pages were drawn up ahead of the election. Some celebrated Donald J. Trump’s unexpected victory. Others, following a majority of predictions at this paper and elsewhere, celebrated Hillary Clinton’s trailblazing triumph.
选举前我们制作了很多头版。有些是庆祝唐纳德·J·特朗普(Donald J. Trump)意料之外的胜利。还有些是根据本报和其他地方的大多数预测，庆祝希拉里·克林顿(Hillary Clinton)的开创性胜利。
And so, in the heart of The Times’s newsroom, long before the exit polls hinted at an upset — and hours before the news media confirmed Mr. Trump’s earthshaking win — Tom Bodkin, The Times’s design director, quietly looked over one such draft.
所以，在时报新闻编辑室的中心地带，在投票站出口民调表明会出现令人失望的结果之前很久——在新闻媒体确认特朗普的震撼性胜利之前数小时——时报的版面设计总监汤姆·博德金(Tom Bodkin)平静地审视这样一个头版。
“MADAM PRESIDENT,” the would-be headline read.
它的标题是“女总统”(Madam President)。
To some, the image you see here is a painful reminder of a historic presidency that did not come to pass.
对有些人来说，此时回想起那一幕，是痛苦地回想起一个没有实现的具有重要历史意义的总统任期。
For others, it speaks volumes about a campaign that many underestimated since the very beginning.
对其他人来说，它清楚地代表着一场从一开始就被很多人低估的选战。
But regardless of one’s political inclination, the image betrays a simple and undeniable truth: Hillary Clinton, who turned 69 last month, will not be America’s first female president. The glass ceiling she strived so ardently to shatter remains, for now, firmly in place.
但是，不管个人政治倾向，那一幕显示了一个简单而不可否认的事实：上个月满69岁的希拉里·克林顿不会成为美国的首位女总统。她努力打破的玻璃天花板依然暂时牢固地存在。
And The Times — along with its readers in the United States and around the world — will have to shelve the idea of a Madam President.
时报以及它在美国和世界各地的读者将不得不暂时搁置女总统的念头。
