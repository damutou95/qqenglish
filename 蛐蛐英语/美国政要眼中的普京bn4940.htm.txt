“I called to congratulate him and hung up the phone thinking he was tough enough to hold Russia together and hoping he was wise enough to find an honorable way out of the Chechnya problem and committed enough to democracy to preserve it.”
“我打电话向他表示祝贺，挂断电话之后心里想，他足够强悍，能把俄罗斯凝聚在一起，希望他有足够的智慧，能为车臣问题找到一个体面的解决方案，也希望他足够地致力于民主，从而愿意维护民主体制。”
President Bill Clinton, writing in “My Life” about Vladimir Putin’s election in March 2000.
比尔·克林顿总统在《我的生活》(My Life)一书中，有关弗拉基米尔·普京(Vladimir Putin)于2000年3月当选总统一事这样写道。
“I looked the man in the eye. I found him to be very straightforward and trustworthy. We had a very good dialogue. I was able to get a sense of his soul.”
“我和他直来直往，发现他直截了当、值得信赖。我们交谈得很愉快。我能感觉到一点儿他内心深处的东西。”
President George W. Bush, after first meeting with Mr. Putin in June 2001.
乔治·W·布什总统(George W. Bush)在2001年6月与普京第一次会面后。
“He’s not well-informed. It’s like arguing with an eighth grader with his facts wrong.”
“他并非见多识广。像是跟一个八年级的学生辩论似的，他有事实错误。”
Mr. Bush to the visiting prime minister of Denmark in June 2006.
2006年6月布什对到访的丹麦首相这样说。
“I think K.G.B., K.G.B., K.G.B.”
“我脑子里想着克格勃、克格勃、克格勃。”
Vice President Dick Cheney, on his impression of Mr. Putin, in private conversations in 2001.
副总统迪克·切尼(Dick Cheney)在2001年的一次私人谈话中，提到对普京的印象。
“I had looked into Putin’s eyes and, just as I expected, had seen a stone-cold killer.”
“我直视普京，正如我所料，我看到了一个冰冷的杀手。”
Robert M. Gates, the defense secretary for Mr. Bush and President Obama, writing in “Duty” about his meeting with Mr. Putin in February 2007.
曾分别担任布什总统和奥巴马总统的国防部长的罗伯特·M·盖茨(Robert M. Gates)在回忆录《职责》一书中，关于2007年2月他与普京的会面。
“I don’t have a bad personal relationship with Putin. When we have conversations, they’re candid, they’re blunt, oftentimes they’re constructive. I know the press likes to focus on body language and he’s got that kind of slouch, looking like the bored kid in the back of the classroom.”
“我与普京的私交不坏。我们交谈时，谈话是坦率的、直言不讳的，而且往往是有建设性的。我知道媒体喜欢关注肢体语言，他有那种懒散相，看起来像是坐在教室后面的无聊孩子。”
Mr. Obama, in a news conference in August 2013.
奥巴马在2013年8月的一次新闻发布会上。
