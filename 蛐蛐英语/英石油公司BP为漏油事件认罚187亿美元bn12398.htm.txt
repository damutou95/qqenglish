NEW ORLEANS — The Gulf Coast states and the federal government have reached a tentative settlement with BP for the British oil company to pay $18.7 billion over 18 years, to compensate for damages from the 2010 Deepwater Horizon oil spill, state officials said Thursday.
美国新奥尔良——州政府官员周四说，墨西哥湾沿岸各州政府以及美国联邦政府已与英国石油公司BP达成初步和解协议，BP将在未来18年里支付187亿美元(约合1160亿元人民币)，以赔偿2010年深水地平线(Deepwater Horizon)钻井平台石油泄漏事故所造成的损害。
“This is a landmark settlement,” Gov. Robert Bentley of Alabama said. “It is designed to compensate the state for all the damages, both environmental and economic.”
“这是一个里程碑式的和解协议，”阿拉巴马州州长罗伯特·本特利(Robert Bentley)说。“它的目的是弥补各州遭受的所有损害，不仅是环境损害，也包括经济损失。”
The settlement covers suits filed by five states — Alabama, Florida, Louisiana, Mississippi and Texas — the federal government, and more 400 local government entities in the Gulf States.
和解协议将把五个州（阿拉巴马州、佛罗里达州、路易斯安那州、密西西比州、得克萨斯州）、联邦政府，以及墨西哥湾各州的400多个地方政府的诉讼案件都包括进来。
BP had already agreed to pay a $4 billion plea agreement for criminal fines in the spill.
此前，BP已在认罪协议中同意为石油泄露事故支付40亿美元的刑事罚款。
