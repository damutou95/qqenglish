Studio Drift, a design group founded by Lonneke Gordijn and Ralph Nauta in Amsterdam, has built its reputation on fluff. A decade ago, Ms. Gordijn began gluing dandelion seeds to LEDs to produce lamps that softly filter light. The most ambitious versions take the form of chandeliers called Fragile Future, where the reconstituted dandelion heads are set in graceful bronze cages.
漂流工作室(Studio Drift)是伦内克·戈尔丁(Lonneke Gordijn)和拉尔夫·诺塔(Ralph Nauta)在阿姆斯特丹成立的一支设计团队，是靠随风飘扬的绒毛成的名。十年前，戈尔丁开始将蒲公英种子粘在LED灯泡上，制作出可以柔和过滤光线的灯具。其最具雄心的灯具设计品，采用了枝形吊灯的形式，命名为“脆弱的未来”(Fragile Future)。在这款灯具中，重新拼贴的蒲公英花盘被固定在了精美的青铜笼子里。
It began when Ms. Gordijn plucked a ready-to-blow dandelion and stuck the seeds to an LED powered by a battery. The desk lamp with brass leaf and stem that resulted is called Dandelight, and after years of being sold primarily through Studio Drift’s website, it now goes for $140 at the MoMA Store.
这一切都始于戈尔丁摘下一株绒毛完全打开的蒲公英，将其种子固定在一盏电池供电式的LED灯上。这盏配有黄铜茎叶、被命名为蒲公英灯(Dandelight)的台灯，多年来主要在漂流工作室的网站上销售；而今，也在现代艺术博物馆(MoMA)的艺术品商店里，以140美元（约合人民币876元）的价格出售了。
